Interface Navigation
Navigate Cinema Studio 3.5
Optical Rig · Genre Panel · Model Selector · Duration · Camera Movement

Cinema Studio 3.5 is organized around a directorial workflow — set up the optical rig first, then choose genre, then write the prompt. This is the opposite of most AI tools. Understanding each panel before generating saves significant credit.

  • 1
    Click Cinema Studio in the main navigation. The studio workspace opens with a central prompt field and control panels on the sides
  • 2
    Find the OPTICAL RIG panel — Cinema Studio's defining feature. Camera body, lens type, focal length, aperture. Set these before writing any prompt
  • 3
    Find the GENRE panel — shapes pacing, motion energy, and visual tone. Available genres for MEDUSA: Horror, Drama, Epic
  • 4
    Find the MODEL SELECTOR — dropdown showing Kling 3.0, Seedance 2.0, Sora 2, WAN 2.5, and others. Each model has different credit cost and visual character
  • 5
    Set Duration: 6 seconds for all MEDUSA website scenes. Aspect ratio: 16:9
  • 6
    Find the CAMERA MOVEMENT panel — Cinema Studio allows stacking up to 3 simultaneous movements. For MEDUSA: Static only (except Scene 03 which uses imperceptible slow dolly)

Why ARRI + Anamorphic for MEDUSA
The ARRI camera body produces the color science and dynamic range of prestige cinema — the same camera used on Arrival, Dune, and Annihilation. The Anamorphic lens creates the distinctive horizontal lens flares and oval bokeh that signals "serious film." Setting these before the prompt means Cinema Studio 3.5 generates Villeneuve-grade optics from the first frame.

Built-In AI Director · Project Brief · How to Direct Each Scene

Mr. Higgs is Cinema Studio 3.5's built-in AI co-director. He understands your project context — characters, locations, style, camera settings — and breaks your description into shots, adjusts settings, and populates prompts in real time. Brief him once at the start of each session.

  • 3
    After briefing, direct him for each scene: "Set up Scene 01 — extreme close-up macro, serpent scales, After World, cold 3200K, Horror genre, Kling 3.0, ARRI Anamorphic 100mm f/2"
  • 4
    Mr. Higgs will populate the optical rig, select genre, choose model, and draft a starting prompt — review and adjust before generating
  • 5

Mr. Higgs Project Brief — Copy and Paste at Session Start
This is MEDUSA — a mythological psychological horror film for Umbra Stream LLC. Directorial register: Denis Villeneuve × Christopher Nolan. The camera worships the world. Nothing moves fast. Dread through stillness and scale, not shock. Every frame should feel like Villeneuve's Arrival or Dune — shot on ARRI with Anamorphic lenses, sacred atmosphere, deep focus, 35mm film grain. Two visual worlds: the Before World (warm 4500K amber, priestess temple, ivory and honey) and the After World (cold 3200K teal, cave system, stone and serpents). All website scenes are After World except Scene 02 which is Before World. Genre map: Scenes 01, 03, 04 = Horror. Scene 02 = Drama. Scene 05 = Epic. Model map: Scenes 01, 02 = Kling 3.0. Scenes 03, 05 = Seedance 2.0. Scene 04 = Sora 2. Camera map: Scenes 01–04 = ARRI Anamorphic. Scene 05 = IMAX Anamorphic. All camera movements must be static or an imperceptible slow dolly only. Duration: 6 seconds per scene. The horror is in the beauty and the stillness.
Mr. Higgs Working Method
Use Mr. Higgs as a shot setup assistant. Tell him what you want in plain language, let him configure the rig and draft a starting prompt. Then replace his draft with the full prompt from this guide before hitting Generate. This approach saves setup time without sacrificing prompt precision.

Elements System
Build Your MEDUSA Elements
@MEDUSA_AFTER · @STONE_GARDEN · Character Lock · Location Lock

Elements are Cinema Studio 3.5's system for locking characters, locations, and props as reusable assets referenced with @tags across every generation. Build these two core Elements before generating any scene that includes Medusa or the stone garden.

  • 1
    In Cinema Studio 3.5, find the Elements panel in the sidebar or dedicated Elements tab
  • 2
    Click Create Element → Character
  • 3
    Upload your approved MEDUSA_REF_AFTER.jpg as the anchor image — this locks Medusa's face, serpents, and costume
  • 4
    Name the Element: @MEDUSA_AFTER — Save. You can now call this character into any generation by typing @MEDUSA_AFTER in the prompt
  • 5
    Click Create Element → Location
  • 6
    Write the location description: "Ancient Mediterranean coastal cave system. Stone figures frozen mid-movement. Cold 3200K teal light. Cave floor covered in thousands of carved owls. Cathedral-scale interior."
  • 7
    Name the Element: @STONE_GARDEN — Save. This location is now callable across all After World scene prompts
Why Elements Are Critical for Scene 03
Without @MEDUSA_AFTER locked from MEDUSA_REF_AFTER.jpg, Cinema Studio 3.5 generates a different-looking character every time. The Elements system is what makes character consistency possible across multiple generations. Build both elements before attempting any scene featuring Medusa or the stone garden.

Extreme macro close-up of living serpents — dark olive-green scales, each scale catching cold blue-teal torchlight from a single source below frame. What a Good Result Looks Like
Scales fill most of the frame. Color is cold blue-teal throughout — no amber anywhere. One serpent lifts and settles with breathing rhythm. If camera moves: add "camera: locked off, absolutely still, zero movement, no shake, no drift" and regenerate. Scene 02 · The Lamp · CS 3.5 · Kling 3.0 · Drama · ARRI Anamorphic 35mm f/1.8
Ancient Greek temple interior at night. A single large oil lamp flame burns alone on a stone altar, filling the chamber with warm amber light — 4500K. Limestone walls. Long shadows behind carved stone columns extending into darkness. An owl motif carved in low relief on the back wall catches the flame's light. The flame burns steady and warm for the first two seconds. Then — at exactly 2 seconds — the flame gutters suddenly, without visible cause, dipping almost to nothing. The room falls near-dark for one full beat. Then the flame recovers fully. The world is warm again. But something has changed. Camera absolutely still. Medium shot centered on the lamp flame. Film grain. Anamorphic lens flare at flame edges — soft, warm. No characters in frame. 4500K warm color grade. Sacred, still atmosphere. Villeneuve Arrival warm aesthetic — ivory, honey, amber. The horror is in the return to the light.

Scene 03 · She Reads Stone · CS 3.5 · Seedance 2.0 · Horror · @MEDUSA_AFTER · @STONE_GARDEN
@MEDUSA_AFTER walks slowly through @STONE_GARDEN at cold dawn, eyes completely closed for the entire duration of the clip — they never open, not even briefly. She moves without looking, navigating every stone figure from memory. She reaches out and places her fingertips gently on the frozen stone face of a soldier, reading his expression the way the blind read text. Living serpents move slowly with each breath and step. The Only Non-Negotiable Rule in MEDUSA Production
If Medusa's eyes open at any point in Scene 03 — reject the clip immediately. Eyes closed = the moral logic of the scene. Worth 5–6 regenerations. If it keeps failing: add "character's eyes are physically closed, eyelids shut, eyes do not open at any moment in the 6-second clip" to the prompt and regenerate.

Full Cinema Studio 3.5 Prompt — Copy Exactly
Scene 04 · The Sea Stops · CS 3.5 · Sora 2 · Horror · ARRI Anamorphic 24mm f/2
Ancient Mediterranean sea surface at night. The water is perfectly, physically impossibly still — no waves, no ripples, no movement of any kind. Mirror-flat. Stars reflected in the black water below as perfectly as they appear above — completely undistorted. The horizon is invisible — sky and sea are the same darkness, one continuous field of absolute black and deep navy. A single distant torch flame on the far shore reflected as one unbroken vertical line — no distortion. Subsurface faint bioluminescence, blue-green, barely visible at depth. Camera at water surface level, looking across. Absolutely still. Cold 3200K. Deep navy and absolute black. No characters. The wrongness is in the perfection. Photorealistic. No digital brightness. No warm tones. Villeneuve Arrival first-contact atmosphere — the silence before something vast arrives. Divine horror through physics failure, not through a visible face.
Camera: ARRI · Anamorphic · 24mm · f/2
Genre: Horror
Model: Sora 2
Motion: Zero — impossible stillness
Why Sora 2 for Scene 04
Sora 2 has the strongest water physics simulation in Cinema Studio 3.5. The mirror-flat ocean — water rendered in a state it physically cannot exist in — is exactly the impossible physical state that Sora 2 handles best. Kling 3.0 may produce visible waves even when instructed not to. Sora 2 is the correct model for Scene 04.

Scene 05 · White Wings in Dark · CS 3.5 · Seedance 2.0 · Epic · IMAX Anamorphic 35mm f/1.8
Interior of a vast ancient cave. Torchlight from below — absolute darkness in the upper two-thirds of frame. Thousands of small owl shapes carved into the stone floor, lit from below in warm orange — the only warmth in the entire frame. Stone human figures frozen mid-movement pressed against cave walls in background. Then — enormous pure white wings rising upward from below frame and filling the entire width of the screen. The wings are dazzling white — the only white object in a world of cold teal and black — backlit from below, each feather luminous. White feathers detach and drift slowly in 50% slow motion, touching cave walls, stone figures, carved owls on the floor. A golden arm barely visible at left frame edge. Camera completely still. Wide shot — IMAX scale. Cold 3200K ambient field with the white wings as the only luminous element. Not triumph — continuation. Something survived that they did not intend. Villeneuve Contact transcendence aesthetic — wonder and dread in the same image.
Camera: IMAX · Anamorphic · 35mm · f/1.8
Genre: Epic
Model: Seedance 2.0
Motion: Wings rising — 50% slow-mo
Why IMAX + Seedance 2.0 for Scene 05
IMAX camera body produces an enormous scale differential — the cave becomes cathedral-sized and the wings become truly overwhelming. Seedance 2.0's physics simulation handles individual feather drift at slow-motion speed with natural air-resistance behavior. Together they make Scene 05 the visual peak of the five scenes.

Review, Approve & Implement
QC Checklist · Download · File Naming · Website Placement Map

Every generated scene must pass the QC checklist before download. Then deploy on the MEDUSA website in the correct sequence and position for maximum impact.

  • ✓
    Optical correctness. ARRI color science present — rich shadow detail, clean highlights. IMAX scale visible in Scene 05. If result looks generic AI, optical rig was not set correctly — reconfigure and regenerate.
  • ✓
    Color temperature correct. Scenes 01, 03, 04: cold 3200K blue-teal, no warmth. Scene 02: warm 4500K amber. Scene 05: pure white against 3200K cold. Wrong color = reject.
  • ✓
    Genre visible in motion energy. Horror scenes: weighted, slow, psychologically pressured. Drama (Sc 02): measured and sacred. Epic (Sc 05): vast and inevitable.
  • ✓
    Scene 03 eyes closed — full 6 seconds. Watch every frame. Any open eyes = immediate reject. Non-negotiable.
  • ✓
    Camera within Villeneuve grammar. Scenes 01, 02, 04, 05: fully static. Scene 03: imperceptible slow dolly only. Any faster motion = reject.
  • ✓
    Atmosphere in 6 seconds. Show to someone unfamiliar. Do they feel dread, warmth, awe, or wrongness without explanation? If nothing: regenerate.
